Most Recommended Tourist Attraction in Siquijor!

Cantabon Cave

Up for a thrilling experience? Cantabon Cave takes you deep into Siquijor’s underground wonders, featuring stalactites, stalagmites, crystal-clear pools, and narrow passageways. It’s an exhilarating adventure that will leave you in awe!

Cambughay Falls

Imagine a three-tiered waterfall cascading into turquoise-blue pools, surrounded by lush jungle. Cambugahay Falls is the perfect place to swim, swing from a vine like Tarzan, or simply relax in the refreshing waters. Its beauty is so mesmerizing, you'll never want to leave!

Paliton Beach

Known for its powdery white sand, crystal-clear waters, and breathtaking sunset views, Paliton Beach is the perfect place to unwind and feel the island’s tranquility. Many call it the “Maldives of Siquijor”, and once you see it, you'll understand why!

Sambulawan Underground River

Step into a mystical cave system where you’ll be greeted by stalactites, stalagmites, and crystal-clear waters flowing through the underground river. The cool, refreshing atmosphere and the serene, otherworldly beauty of the cave will leave you in awe.

Kagusuan Beach

For those seeking peace and solitude, Kagusuan Beach is a hidden gem. With rock formations, soft sand, and crystal-clear waters, it’s a secret paradise waiting to be discovered.

Exciting Activities to Do in Siquijor!

Swim and Cliff Dive at Salagdoong Beach

For adrenaline junkies, Salagdoong Beach is the perfect spot to cliff dive from towering platforms into crystal-clear waters. If jumping isn’t your thing, just relax on the powdery white sand!

Swing Into Cambugahay Falls

Feel like Tarzan as you swing from a rope into the refreshing turquoise pools of Cambugahay Falls. You can also swim, explore the different levels of the falls, or simply unwind in nature’s beauty.

Ride a Motorbike Around the Island

The best way to explore Siquijor is on two wheels! Rent a motorbike and cruise along the scenic coastal roads, passing by beaches, waterfalls, and charming villages.

Watch the Sunset at Paliton Beach

Dubbed the "Maldives of Siquijor," Paliton Beach offers breathtaking sunset views. Sit back, relax, and enjoy the stunning colors of the sky while feeling the soft sand between your toes.

Discover the Sambulawan Underground River

Explore the hidden Sambulawan Underground River, where you can take a boat ride through mysterious cave chambers filled with stalactites, stalagmites, and crystal-clear waters.

Snorkeling & Diving Adventures

Siquijor’s marine life is spectacular! Grab your snorkel and explore Tubod Marine Sanctuary, where you’ll find colorful coral reefs, sea turtles, and vibrant fish. Check out the stunning ocean!

How do I get there?


Siquijor is accessible via ferry from multiple ports:

From Dumaguete: Take a ferry from Dumaguete Port (around 1 hour).

From Cebu: Take a bus to Liloan or a ferry to Dumaguete, then transfer to a Siquijor-bound ferry.

From Bohol: Catch a fast craft from Tagbilaran (about 1.5–2 hours).

From Manila: Fly to Dumaguete or Cebu, then take a ferry.

By Air: Fly to Dumaguete (Sibulan Airport), the closest airport to Siquijor.

By Sea: Take a ferry from Dumaguete, Cebu, or Bohol to Siquijor Port or Larena Port.

By Land & Sea: From Cebu, take a bus to Liloan Port (Santander), then a ferry to Dumaguete, followed by a ferry to Siquijor.

What is Siquijor famous for?

Siquijor is known for its stunning beaches, waterfalls, caves, and mystical traditions. It’s famous for its folk healing practices, enchanted balete tree, and the Healing Festival.

When is the best time to visit Siquijor?

The best time is during the dry season (November to May) when the weather is sunny and ideal for island-hopping and exploring.
